OUR TEAM

Our lab team is committed to advancing scientific discovery through precision, innovation, and collaboration, delivering high-quality results and contributing to groundbreaking research.

Hugo F. Monteiro
BSc Animal Science, PhD, Postdoc​

Dr. Hugo Monteiro is a Postdoctoral Scholar at UC Davis' School of Veterinary Medicine, with extensive international experience. Originally from Brazil, he earned his bachelor's in Animal Sciences and completed his Ph.D. at the University of Florida, specializing in ruminant nutrition and rumen microbiology. His research focuses on enhancing dairy cow production, reproduction, and health using Bioinformatics and AI.

Saleh Salman
BSc Animal Science, MSc​, PhD Student

Saleh graduated a BSc in Animal Production in 2012, a MSc coursework in 2015 from Assiut University, Egypt. Then a MSc of Animal Sciences (Equine Reproduction) from Colorado State University, in 2019 as a Fulbrighter. He had a stint at UCONN in Dr. Tang and Tian labs studying Bovine bIPS and Proteomics & Metabolomics of follicular fluid. Currently, Saleh is pursuing his PhD at University of California, Davis in Dr. Lima Lab studying NGF, AMH, and Inhibins roles in reproduction performance.

Tana Jo Almand
BSc Animal Science, MSc​, PhD Student

Raised on a beef calf and dairy farm in Northeastern Oklahoma, she earned degrees in Animal Science while competing on livestock judging teams. She studied male porcine reproduction at UC Davis during her master's and managed a milk quality lab in California's Central Valley. Currently, she is pursuing a Ph.D. focused on mastitis and selective dry cow programs in dairy cows.

Adriana Garzon
DVM, MSc Veterinary Medicine, PhD, Livestock Herd Health and Reproduction Resident

Dr. Adriana Garzon, a DVM from Universidad Nacional de Colombia, furthered her expertise with a Master’s in Animal Health and a PhD in Animal Biology from UC Davis. She is currently pursuing a Master in Preventive Veterinary Medicine at UC Davis. Her research focuses on using epidemiological and genomic tools to reduce disease transmission and combat antimicrobial resistance in dairy cattle. She is currently studying Mannheimia haemolytica and Pasteurella multocida in dairy cattle with Bovine Respiratory Disease at Pereira's Lab.

Maria Julia Wollentarski
DVM, Graduate Student​

Maria Julia, a graduate student in Dr. Lima’s lab at UC Davis, earned her DVM from Anhembi Morumbi University in Brazil in 2022. After gaining experience on a dairy farm in Denmark, she joined Dr. Lima’s lab as a research assistant in 2023, focusing on cow nutrition and reproduction. In 2024, she began her master’s program, continuing research in reproduction, physiology, and endocrinology to improve cattle health and productivity.

Craig Miramontes
BSc Animal Science, Assistant Specialist

As an Assistant Specialist in the Pereira's Lab, my duties include assisting with grant, manuscript, and lab protocol preparation; updating and maintaining lab training records, lab inventory, and lab chemical inventory; packing for and assisting with field sample collection such as milk, fecal, and deep nasal swabs; conducting various microbiological work such as DNA extraction, plating bacteria, minimum inhibitory concentration testing; communicating with producers and administering surveys.

Maria Clara G. M. da Silva
DVM, Research Assistant

Originally from Brazil, Maria Clara earned her DVM from University Center Jaguariúna in São Paulo (2021) and gained two years of hands-on experience in clinical practice, surgery, and dairy calf management. Her interest in herd health and disease prevention led her to pursue research opportunities. Maria Clara joined Dr. Lima’s lab at UC Davis where she focuses on improving reproductive efficiency and population health strategies in dairy herds to enhance cattle health, productivity, and sustainability.



Akihiro Ueda
DVM, Research Assistant

Akihiro Ueda earned his DVM from Kitasato University in Japan in 2022. He began his research career as a Research Assistant at Purdue University’s College of Veterinary Medicine, focusing on Dairy Cattle Health. He then pursued a Clinical Internship in Farm Animal Reproduction, Medicine, and Surgery at the University of Illinois. Currently, he is a Research Assistant in Dr. Lima’s Lab at UC Davis, contributing to research on reproduction and population health to enhance dairy cow welfare.
