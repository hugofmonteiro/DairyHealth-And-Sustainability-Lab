Alumni

Our alumni are key to our legacy, contributing to science, industry, and academia. They continue to drive innovation and serve as mentors, sharing their expertise to inspire the next generation of leaders.

Thaisa Marques
DVM, MSc, PhD, PostDoc

Dr. Thaisa Marques, from Brazil, earned her DVM, Master’s, and Ph.D. in Veterinary Medicine. With 18 years of experience as a livestock consultant and veterinarian, she specializes in bovine embryo production, reproductive management, and data analysis from cow sensors. Currently at UC Davis, she focuses on automatic milk systems, nutritional interventions, and advancements in embryo development. Dr. Marques combines extensive knowledge and practical expertise to improve cattle health and reproduction.


Eduardo Oliveira
DVM, MSc, PhD

Eduardo earned his DVM from the Federal University of Goias, Brazil, and gained experience in dairy reproduction at the University of Illinois. After working as an associate veterinarian at Alta Genetics, Brazil, he completed a master’s at the University of Florida and a residency in dairy production medicine at UC Davis. His research focuses on uterine disease in dairy cows and antimicrobial stewardship. Eduardo now works as a Dairy Production Specialist for Zoetis Animal Health in Northern California, Nevada, and Utah.

Thadeu De Castro
DVM, PhD, Theriogenology Resident II at UC Davis School of Veterinary Medicine

Thadeu De Castro earned his DVM in 2014 from the State University of Northern Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He holds a master's and Ph.D. in veterinary medicine, specializing in large animal reproduction. His research, conducted in collaboration with the University of Wisconsin and the Eutheria Foundation, focuses on reproductive biology and biotechnology in cattle and horses.




Delaine Mello
DVM, MSc Veterinary Medicine 

She is originally from Brazil, where she earned her DVM at Pontificia
Catholic University in Minas Gerais. She has a vast experience in reproduction physiology
and management of beef and dairy cows and worked for 13 years as a Veterinary
Practitioner in Sao Paulo, Brazil. Delaine came to the U.S. in 2021 and worked as a research
assistant at UC Davis in Dr. Lima’s lab performing different research focusing on the
transition period and reproduction of dairy cows. Delaine master’s research is focusing on
improving reproduction in dairy cows through managing different approaches in timed AI
reproductive protocols

Wilson Coelho
DVM, MSc Animal Science​

Wilson Coelho Jr. was a master’s student at UC Davis' Lima Lab, with a degree in Animal Science from Sao Paulo State University (2019). He gained early experience with dairy cows in nutrition, reproduction, and management at Conapec Jr. and later worked with Dr. Lima at the University of Illinois, contributing to five trials on dairy cow reproduction, health, and rumen microbiome. Wilson’s research focuses on improving feed efficiency using biomarkers from ruminal fermentation.

Isabelle Macedo
DVM, MsC Veterinary Medicine, Intern at UC Davis School of Veterinary Medicine

Isabelle Matos earned her DVM from the Federal University of Goias in Brazil. She gained experience in microbiology, immunology, and epidemiology of dairy cows during an externship at Cornell University in Dr. Rodrigo Bicalho's Lab. After returning to Brazil, Isabelle worked as a cattle veterinarian while pursuing her master's under Dra. Maria Lucia Gambarini, with Dr. Lima as co-advisor. She is currently undertaking an Ambulatory Internship at UC Davis, focusing on transitional period, uterine health, milk quality, and reproductive performance.
