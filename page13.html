3D Imaging for Mastitis Detection 

About The Project

Our laboratory is currently exploring an innovative approach to the early detection and diagnosis of mastitis, a common and costly condition affecting dairy cows. We are utilizing cutting-edge 3D imaging technology, specifically the Intel RealSense camera, to create detailed, high-resolution models of the udder, allowing for more accurate analysis and monitoring.

We are excited about the possibilities this technology offers in advancing precision livestock farming and contributing to a more sustainable and efficient dairy industry.

Mastitis, an infection of the udder tissue, can significantly impact milk production and quality, posing major challenges to the dairy industry. Traditional methods of detecting mastitis rely on visual inspection, palpation, and somatic cell counts, which can be time-consuming and may not provide early indications of the disease. By incorporating advanced 3D imaging techniques, our project aims to improve the precision and efficiency of mastitis detection.

The Intel RealSense camera captures depth and texture data, enabling the creation of 3D visualizations of the udder. These models provide detailed information about tissue changes, which can be an early indicator of mastitis. Our team is developing algorithms to analyze these 3D images, identifying subtle variations in the tissue that may not be visible through conventional methods.

This research has the potential to revolutionize the way veterinarians and dairy farmers monitor udder health, offering a non-invasive, real-time tool to detect mastitis before it affects milk production or the health of the animal. In the future, this technology could be integrated into automated systems, enabling continuous, real-time monitoring of dairy herds, leading to more effective disease management and improved animal welfare.
