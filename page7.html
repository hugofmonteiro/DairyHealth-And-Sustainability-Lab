About Our Publications 

Infectious Disease

Our population health research focuses on the overall health and well-being of cattle populations. We study disease transmission, environmental stressors, and management practices impacting herd health. Using epidemiological methods, we identify disease risks and develop prevention strategies. Our goal is to enhance herd health through early detection, vaccinations, and biosecurity measures. This research ensures sustainable dairy farming while improving animal welfare and productivity.

Reproduction Health and Technology

Our research focuses on improving fertility and reproductive efficiency in dairy cattle. We examine physiological, genetic, and environmental factors affecting reproduction to optimize breeding strategies. By studying hormonal regulation and advanced breeding techniques, we aim to enhance conception rates and reduce calving intervals. Our work addresses reproductive challenges like anestrus and subfertility. Ultimately, we seek sustainable solutions to improve herd management and productivity.

Microbial Genomics and Nutrition

Our nutrition research focuses on understanding how diet influences the health, performance, and reproductive success of dairy cattle. We investigate the link between nutrient intake, metabolism, and milk production, aiming to improve feed efficiency and optimize diets for various stages of lactation and growth. By studying the impact of different feed ingredients, additives, and feeding strategies, we strive to enhance milk yield, fertility, and overall cattle health while preventing diseases like ketosis and mastitis.
